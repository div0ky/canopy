# Governance

Doxa currently uses an owner-led governance model. It is independently owned and maintained by
[div0ky](https://github.com/div0ky); Midtown Home Improvements is its sole supported consumer and
does not own or maintain the framework.

Maintainers are responsible for roadmap direction, accepted architectural decisions, release
quality, security response, and stewardship of the package and repository namespaces. Decisions are
recorded through pull requests and the manifesto when they affect the published architecture. The
supported adoption program and development roadmap remain closed; public package availability does
not create roadmap participation or support eligibility.

## Decision process

- Routine fixes and documentation changes are decided through pull-request review.
- Public API, lifecycle, persistence, security, packaging, or compatibility changes require an
  accepted decision record.
- Maintainers seek useful review. The owner makes and documents the final decision.
- Security embargoes may be handled privately until coordinated disclosure.

## Becoming a maintainer

Maintainers are invited based on sustained, technically sound contributions; respectful community
participation; sound judgment around compatibility and security; and demonstrated stewardship beyond
a single feature.

Governance will be revised before responsibility is distributed beyond the initial maintainer.
